💡 What is EMI? Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is the fixed monthly payment you make to repay a loan. It includes both principal and interest components.
📌 EMI Formula EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / [(1+r)^n – 1]
where P = Principal, r = Monthly Rate, n = Tenure in months
🏦 Repo Rate vs Lending Rate The RBI Repo Rate (6.50%) is the rate at which banks borrow from RBI. Your actual loan rate will typically be Repo Rate + Bank Spread (usually 2–5%).
⚡ EMI vs EPI EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) keeps the monthly payment fixed throughout the tenure. EPI (Equated Principal Instalment) keeps principal repayment fixed, so interest reduces each month.

💡 Types of Overdraft (OD) Facilities

FD OD (FDOD)

Overdraft against Fixed Deposit. Typically 80–90% of FD value. Lowest interest rates (usually FD Rate + 1–2%). Most secure form of OD.

Stock/Book Debt OD (CC)

Cash Credit backed by hypothecation of stocks and debtors. 60–80% of drawing power allowed. Requires monthly stock statements.

Swap OD

Short-term OD swapped regularly between banks or against inward remittances/receivables. Used by traders and exporters for working capital.

Credit Guarantee

Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS)

Ministry of Finance | 100% Government Guarantee

About the Scheme

ECLGS provides 100% guarantee coverage on additional credit to MSMEs, business enterprises, and individuals (for business purposes) impacted by COVID-19 and other disruptions. The scheme is implemented through Scheduled Commercial Banks, NBFCs, and MFIs.

Key Benefits

  • Additional working capital term loan of up to 20% of outstanding credit (subject to limits)
  • Maximum loan: ₹5 crore per borrower
  • Interest rate capped at 9.25% p.a. for banks; 14% for NBFCs
  • No guarantee fee on scheme
  • Moratorium of 12 months on principal repayment
  • 4-year repayment tenure

Eligibility Criteria

  • MSME / Business with outstanding credit up to ₹50 crore as of 29 Feb 2020
  • Account should be Standard (not NPA) as of 29 Feb 2020
  • Udyam Registration is beneficial (not always mandatory)
  • GST registered entities preferred

Credit Guarantee

CGTMSE — Collateral Free Loans for MSEs

MSME Ministry + SIDBI | Collateral & Third Party Guarantee Free

About the Scheme

CGTMSE enables micro and small enterprises (MSEs) to avail collateral and third-party guarantee-free credit from Member Lending Institutions (MLIs). The trust provides guarantee to MLIs for credit extended to MSEs, reducing the risk of lending and enabling formal credit access.

Key Benefits

  • No collateral or third-party guarantee required
  • Maximum credit coverage: ₹5 crore
  • 85% coverage for micro enterprises (up to ₹5 lakh)
  • 75% coverage for loans above ₹50 lakh
  • Special 80% coverage for women entrepreneurs
  • Available through 100+ Member Lending Institutions

Eligibility Criteria

  • Micro or Small Enterprise (not Medium)
  • Udyam Registration mandatory
  • Account must be Standard
  • Annual Turnover within MSME classification limits
  • New or existing business eligible

Overdraft (OD) Facility

Working Capital | Revolving Credit

What is Overdraft?

An Overdraft (OD) is a revolving credit facility where your bank account is linked to a sanctioned credit limit. You can withdraw funds up to this limit and interest is charged only on the daily outstanding balance utilized, not the full sanctioned amount.

How It Works

  • Bank sanctions a limit (e.g., ₹50 lakh) against security or based on creditworthiness
  • You can draw and repay multiple times within the limit
  • Interest charged daily on utilized amount at annual rate / 365
  • Annual renewal of limit by submitting fresh financials

Secured vs Unsecured OD

  • Secured: Against FD, property, stocks, debtors — lower interest rate
  • Unsecured: Clean OD based on business vintage, turnover, and credit score — higher rate

Types of OD

  • FD OD (FDOD) — against fixed deposit; cheapest option
  • Cash Credit (CC) — against stocks/debtors; most common for traders
  • Book Debt OD — against outstanding receivables
  • Swap OD — short-term, rolled over against expected receivables
  • Property OD / LAP OD — against residential or commercial property
💡 When to use OD Best suited for businesses with seasonal cash flows, fluctuating working capital needs, or quick-payment requirements. Avoid using OD for long-term capital expenditure.

Term Loan

CAPEX / MTL | Fixed Tenure Repayment

What is a Term Loan?

A Term Loan is a lump-sum credit disbursed for a specific purpose and repaid over a fixed period through regular instalments. The repayment schedule is fixed at the start — either EMI (Equated Monthly Instalments) or EPI (Equated Principal Instalments).

EMI vs EPI

  • EMI: Fixed monthly payment throughout. Higher initial interest portion, declining over time. Simple to plan.
  • EPI: Fixed principal each month + reducing interest. Monthly payment decreases each month. Lower total interest outgo.

Types of Term Loans for MSMEs

  • CAPEX Loan: For purchase of machinery, equipment, and fixed assets. Long tenure (3–10 years). Assets financed serve as primary security.
  • Medium-Term Loan (MTL): For business expansion, renovation, or working capital gaps. 1–5 year tenure.
  • Property Purchase Loan: For buying commercial/industrial property for business use. Up to 15–20 years. Usually 75–80% LTV.
Secured vs Unsecured? Most term loans are secured against the asset being financed. CGTMSE can be used to make them collateral-free. Unsecured term loans (clean loans) available from Fintechs at higher rates.

Letter of Credit (LC)

Trade Finance | Bank-Backed Payment Guarantee

What is an LC?

A Letter of Credit (LC) is a bank instrument where the buyer's bank (issuing bank) guarantees payment to the seller (beneficiary) upon presentation of specified documents proving shipment or delivery. It eliminates credit risk between trading parties who don't know each other well.

How It Works

  • Buyer applies to their bank for LC issuance
  • Bank issues LC in favour of seller specifying terms and documents
  • Seller ships goods and presents documents to their bank
  • Seller's bank (negotiating bank) pays seller and claims from issuing bank
  • Issuing bank debits buyer's account

Types of LC

  • Sight LC — payment on demand upon document presentation
  • Usance LC — deferred payment (30/60/90/180 days)
  • Standby LC (SBLC) — payment guarantee on default
  • Revolving LC — for repeat transactions

Bank Guarantee (BG)

Non-Fund Based | Contractual Assurance

What is a Bank Guarantee?

A Bank Guarantee is a bank's commitment to pay a specified amount to a beneficiary if the applicant (your business) fails to fulfil its contractual obligations. Unlike an LC, BG is a secondary (contingent) liability — invoked only on default.

Common Uses for MSMEs

  • Tender / Bid BG — to participate in government tenders (EMD)
  • Performance BG — to assure completion of project/order
  • Advance Payment BG — when buyer pays advance, seller gives BG
  • Financial BG — guarantee of loan repayment to another party

How to Avail

  • Apply to your bank with details of the BG requirement
  • Bank will ask for 100% cash margin OR allow against existing credit limit
  • Charges: Usually 0.5–1% p.a. on BG amount + stamp duty

Working Capital Term Loan (WCTL)

Working Capital | Fixed Tenure

About WCTL

A WCTL is a term loan sanctioned specifically to fund working capital deficits. Unlike CC/OD which are revolving, WCTL is disbursed in one shot and repaid over a fixed schedule of 1–5 years through EMI or EPI.

Key Features

  • Tenure: 12 to 60 months (typically)
  • Security: Primary — hypothecation of current assets. Collateral may be needed for larger amounts.
  • Used to bridge working capital gap permanently (not revolving)
  • Interest: Floating, linked to bank's MCLR/Repo Rate

Lease Rental Discounting (LRD)

Commercial RE | Rental-Backed Loan

About LRD

LRD is a loan product where a business owner pledges future lease rental receivables from their tenants as security. The bank discounts these future cash flows and provides a lump-sum loan. EMIs are serviced directly from tenant payments via NACH/ECS.

Key Features

  • Loan = 70–80% of total lease rental receivables discounted at bank's rate
  • Lower interest rates vs LAP (since cash flows are predictable)
  • Tenure tied to lease agreement duration
  • Tenant creditworthiness is key factor

LC Discounting

Trade Finance | Immediate Liquidity

About LC Discounting

LC Discounting allows a seller (beneficiary of an LC) to get immediate payment by selling the LC receivable to a bank at a discount. Instead of waiting for the LC maturity date (say 90 days), the seller gets funds immediately minus a discounting charge.

Benefits

  • Immediate liquidity — no need to wait for LC payment date
  • Off-balance sheet financing for buyer
  • Discounting rates typically lower than CC/OD rates
  • Reduces credit risk since LC is bank-backed

Cash Credit (CC)

Working Capital | Stock & Debtor Backed

About Cash Credit

Cash Credit is a short-term revolving credit backed by hypothecation of stocks (inventory) and book debts (receivables). It's the most common working capital facility for manufacturers and traders in India. The maximum you can draw (Drawing Power) is calculated monthly based on stock and debtor statements.

How Drawing Power is Calculated

  • Drawing Power = (Eligible Stock × Margin %) + (Eligible Debtors × Margin %)
  • Typical margins: Stocks — 25–40%; Debtors — 25–40%
  • Submit monthly stock statements to maintain DP
  • Total cannot exceed sanctioned CC limit

Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC)

International Trade | Contingent Guarantee

About SBLC

An SBLC is a payment guarantee issued by a bank at a buyer's request, payable only when the buyer fails to fulfil contractual obligations. It acts as a "last resort" payment guarantee in international trade, distinct from a regular LC which is a primary payment mechanism.

Key Uses

  • International contracts requiring performance assurance
  • Real estate transactions
  • Credit enhancement for cross-border borrowing
  • Oil & gas, infrastructure, and large commodity trades

LER — Loan Equivalent Risk

Risk Metric | Off-Balance Sheet Exposure

About LER

LER (Loan Equivalent Risk) is a banking metric used to convert off-balance sheet exposures (like guarantees, LC, derivatives) into equivalent on-balance sheet loan amounts for risk calculation purposes. Banks use LER for capital adequacy and large exposure framework (LEF) compliance under RBI guidelines.

Why It Matters for MSMEs

  • BGs and LCs issued to your business count towards bank's exposure limits
  • High LER may limit further credit availability from the same bank
  • Affects your bank's assessment of total exposure under LEF

Commercial LAP (Loan Against Property)

Secured Loan | Commercial / Industrial Property

About Commercial LAP

A Commercial LAP is a secured loan against commercial or industrial property (shop, office, warehouse, factory). It offers larger loan amounts and longer tenures compared to personal/residential LAP, making it ideal for MSME business expansion or capital infusion.

Key Features

  • LTV: Typically 55–65% of commercial property value
  • Tenure: Up to 15 years
  • Interest: Lower than unsecured loans; typically MCLR + 1–3%
  • Usage: Business expansion, refinancing existing debt, working capital
  • Secured — property mortgage is mandatory

Secured vs Unsecured

  • Secured — property mortgage to the bank
  • Lower rate than OD/CC/unsecured loans
  • Risk: Property can be auctioned if loan defaults

📖 Import Export Code (IEC) Guide

Your passport to international trade

What is IEC?

Importer Exporter Code (IEC) is a 10-digit code mandatory for any business engaging in import or export of goods or services from/to India. Issued by DGFT (Directorate General of Foreign Trade).

Documents Required

  • PAN of firm/company
  • Aadhaar / Passport / Voter ID of applicant
  • Cancelled cheque or bank certificate
  • Business address proof

Process

  • Visit dgft.gov.in → Login → Services → IEC
  • Fill ANF 2A form online
  • Pay ₹500 fee online
  • IEC issued within 2–3 working days
  • Must update IEC profile annually on DGFT portal

📖 LEI — Legal Entity Identifier Guide

Mandatory for large corporate borrowers

What is LEI?

LEI is a 20-character globally unique identifier for legal entities participating in financial transactions. The RBI has mandated LEI for all entities with aggregate fund-based and non-fund-based credit exposure of ₹5 crore and above.

Applicability

  • Aggregate credit exposure ≥ ₹50 crore: Mandatory since April 2021
  • ₹25–50 crore: Phased implementation
  • From April 2023: All borrowers with exposure ≥ ₹5 crore
  • Annual renewal required; lapsed LEI can block loan renewals

📖 How to Choose the Right Bank

Key factors for MSME banking decisions

Key Evaluation Parameters

  • Interest Rates: Compare base rate, MCLR, Repo-linked rates across banks
  • Processing Fees: 0.5–2% of loan amount; negotiate for waivers
  • MSME Schemes: Some banks have dedicated MSME verticals with faster processing
  • Digital Banking: Robust net banking, NEFT/RTGS, e-statements, API banking
  • Relationship Banking: Dedicated RM for faster query resolution and renewals
  • Credit Appraisal Speed: PSU banks slower (4–8 weeks) vs private/NBFCs (1–3 weeks)
  • Geographic Reach: Important for multi-location businesses or those importing/exporting
💡 Pro Tip Maintain accounts with at least 2 banks — one PSU for lower-cost credit and government scheme access, one private bank for faster service and digital features.
