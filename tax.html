Tax & Certificates

Navigate India's tax landscape — income tax regimes, GST schemes, TDS/TCS rates, and all certificates your MSME needs to stay compliant.

📌 Choose Wisely for FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26) New Tax Regime is the default from FY 2023-24. To choose Old Regime, you must specifically opt for it before or while filing your ITR. Salaried employees must inform their employer at the start of the year. Businesses must file Form 10-IEA.

📌 TDS vs TCS TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) = Deductor pays tax on behalf of payee when making payment above threshold. TCS (Tax Collected at Source) = Seller collects tax from buyer at point of sale. Both are advance tax collection mechanisms — credited to the payee's Form 26AS and adjusted against final tax liability.

Udyam Certificate

Mandatory for MSME identity and access to all schemes
Portal: udyamregistration.gov.in | Free
Permanent validity; update on change in classification, PAN, or business details
Based on investment in P&M and annual turnover — both criteria must be met
Enables priority sector lending, CGTMSE cover, 25% govt procurement reservation
🧾

GST Certificate

Mandatory above ₹40L (goods) / ₹20L (services) turnover
Portal: gst.gov.in
Unique 15-digit GSTIN — mandatory on all tax invoices
Enables ITC claims and inter-state sales
Display prominently at business premises
🥗

FSSAI License

Mandatory for all food businesses — restaurant, manufacture, trade, import
Portal: foscos.fssai.gov.in
Basic (<₹12L), State (₹12L–₹20Cr), Central (>₹20Cr)
Annual renewal required (apply 30 days before expiry)
14-digit FSSAI number must appear on all food packaging
🌍

IEC — Importer Exporter Code

Mandatory for all import/export activities
Portal: dgft.gov.in | Fee: ₹500
Lifetime validity — but annual profile update mandatory (April–June)
One IEC per PAN across all businesses
Required for FEMA compliance and customs clearance
🔢

LEI — Legal Entity Identifier

Mandatory for borrowers with aggregate exposure ≥ ₹5 crore
Portal: leil.in | Fee: ₹2,950 + GST/year
Annual renewal — expired LEI may block loan disbursements
20-character globally unique identifier
™️

Trademark Certificate

Protects brand name, logo, tagline from infringement
Portal: ipindia.gov.in | Fee: ₹4,500–₹9,000
™ symbol from filing date; ® symbol after registration
Valid for 10 years; renewable indefinitely
50% fee concession for MSMEs and individuals
🏪

Shops & Establishments Certificate

Mandatory for all commercial establishments — state law
Register within 30 days of commencing business
Apply via state Labour Department portal
Annual renewal required in most states
🏭

Factory License / Registration

Mandatory if: 10+ workers with power / 20+ without power (Factories Act)
Plan approval from Chief Inspector of Factories before construction
Annual renewal; half-yearly returns mandatory
Apply through state Factory Inspector's office
📌 Which ITR Form for Your MSME? The right ITR form depends on your business structure, turnover, and income sources. Filing the wrong form leads to defective return notice from IT Department.
ITR FormWho Should FileKey Features
ITR-1 (Sahaj)Individuals with salary + 1 house property + interest income; total income ≤ ₹50LSimplest form; no business income allowed
ITR-2Individuals with capital gains, multiple house properties, foreign income/assets; no business incomeNo business income; covers most investment scenarios
ITR-3Individuals/HUF with business or professional income NOT under presumptive schemeFull P&L and Balance Sheet required; books of accounts mandatory
ITR-4 (Sugam)Individuals/HUF/Firms with presumptive income under Sec 44AD, 44ADA, 44AE; turnover up to limitsMost MSME-friendly; no balance sheet needed
ITR-5Partnership Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIsP&L and Balance Sheet required; no audit of capital gains
ITR-6Companies (Pvt Ltd, OPC, Public) — except companies claiming exemption u/s 11Detailed financials; mandatory digital filing
ITR-7Entities filing u/s 139(4A)/(4B)/(4C)/(4D) — trusts, political parties, educational institutionsFor charitable / religious / political organisations
💡 For Most Small MSMEs (Proprietorships): ITR-4 (Sugam) is the simplest — if turnover is below ₹3 crore and you opt for presumptive taxation (44AD). You just need basic income/expense summary — no full balance sheet required.
