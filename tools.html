💡 What is EMI?
Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is a fixed monthly payment comprising both principal and interest. It allows borrowers to repay loans in predictable, manageable chunks over the loan tenure.
📐 EMI Formula
EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / [(1+r)ⁿ – 1]
P = Principal | r = Monthly Rate (annual/12/100) | n = Tenure in months
🏦 EMI vs EPI
  • EMI: Fixed payment throughout. Simpler for budgeting.
  • EPI: Fixed principal + reducing interest. Lower total interest. Monthly outflow decreases each month.

Types of Overdraft / CC Facilities

FD OD (FDOD)

Overdraft against Fixed Deposit. Get 80–90% of FD value as OD limit. Cheapest rate available — typically FD Rate + 1–2%. No income proof needed; FD is sole security. Ideal for those with idle FDs.

Lowest RateFD as Security

Cash Credit (CC)

Backed by hypothecation of stocks (inventory) and book debts (receivables). Drawing Power (DP) computed monthly on stock statements. Most common working capital facility for manufacturers and traders.

Stock/Debtor BackedMonthly Statements

Swap OD

Short-term OD rolled over periodically against inward remittances, receivables, or LC proceeds. Popular with exporters and traders for bridging short payment gaps. Usually 30–90 days.

Short TermExport/Trade

Property OD / LAP OD

OD facility against residential or commercial property. Higher limits available. Rate slightly higher than FD OD but lower than unsecured OD. Useful for property-owning businesses needing revolving credit.

Property SecurityHigher Limits

📌 IGST vs CGST/SGST — When to Apply Which?
  • Intra-State Supply (seller and buyer in same state): Charge CGST (50%) + SGST/UTGST (50%)
  • Inter-State Supply (seller and buyer in different states): Charge IGST (full GST rate)
  • Export: Zero-rated supply — no GST. File LUT (Letter of Undertaking) or pay IGST and claim refund.
