Credit Guarantee • Ministry of Finance

Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS)

100% Guarantee Coverage | No Collateral | Up to ₹5 Crore

About the Scheme

ECLGS was launched as part of Aatmanirbhar Bharat package to provide guaranteed credit to MSMEs impacted by economic stress. It enables banks and NBFCs to provide additional credit without taking fresh collateral, with NCGTC (National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company) providing 100% guarantee coverage.

Key Benefits

  • Additional working capital term loan of up to 20% of outstanding credit as of 29 Feb 2020
  • Maximum loan cap: ₹5 crore per borrower
  • Interest rate capped: 9.25% p.a. (banks), 14% p.a. (NBFCs)
  • No processing fee or guarantee fee on borrower
  • 12-month moratorium on principal repayment
  • 4-year overall repayment tenure
  • No fresh collateral required

Eligibility

  • MSME / Business enterprise with credit outstanding ≤ ₹50 crore as of 29 Feb 2020
  • Account should be Standard (not NPA) as on 29 Feb 2020
  • Udyam Registration beneficial; GST registration preferred
  • Individual loans for business purposes also covered up to ₹50 crore
  • Hospitality, travel, leisure, and sports sectors: separate sub-scheme available

Credit Guarantee • MSME Ministry + SIDBI

CGTMSE — Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for MSEs

Collateral Free | Third Party Guarantee Free | Up to ₹5 Crore

About the Scheme

CGTMSE was set up jointly by the Government of India and SIDBI to provide guarantee to Member Lending Institutions (MLIs) for credit extended to MSEs without any collateral or third-party guarantee. This enables MSEs — especially first-generation entrepreneurs without assets — to access formal credit.

Coverage Guarantee Levels

CategoryMax Guarantee Coverage
Micro Enterprises (up to ₹5L)85%
Women Entrepreneurs80%
NE Region / ZED Certified80%
General (above ₹50L)75%
All categories — max credit₹5 Crore

Eligibility

  • Micro or Small Enterprise only (not Medium)
  • Valid Udyam Registration mandatory
  • Account must be Standard (not NPA)
  • New and existing businesses both eligible
  • Retail trade, educational institutions, SHGs, agricultural activities: not eligible

Startup Support • DPIIT

Startup India Initiative

Tax Benefits | Self-Certification | Fund Access | Easy Wind-up

DPIIT Recognition Benefits

  • Income Tax exemption for 3 consecutive years out of first 10 years (Sec 80-IAC)
  • Capital Gains Tax exemption on investments (Sec 54EE) up to ₹50 lakh
  • Self-certification under 9 environmental and labour laws for 3–5 years
  • Fast-track patent application — 80% rebate on patent fees
  • 80% rebate on trademark fees
  • Access to Fund of Funds (₹10,000 crore SIDBI managed)
  • Government procurement: no prior turnover requirement
  • Wind-up business within 90 days under IBC

Eligibility for DPIIT Recognition

  • Incorporated as: Private Ltd Company, LLP, or Registered Partnership Firm
  • Age: Not more than 10 years from date of incorporation
  • Annual turnover: Not exceeding ₹100 crore in any previous financial year
  • Nature: Must be working towards innovation, development, deployment, or commercialisation of new products/services/processes driven by technology or intellectual property
  • Not formed by splitting or reconstructing an existing business

How to Apply

  • Visit startupindia.gov.in → Sign up → Apply for DPIIT Recognition
  • Fill application with company details, sector, and problem statement
  • Upload incorporation certificate and brief description of innovation
  • Recognition granted within 2–5 working days (mostly)

Employment Generation • KVIC

PM Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP)

15–35% Margin Money Subsidy | New Micro Enterprises

About PMEGP

PMEGP is a credit-linked subsidy programme for setting up new micro enterprises in manufacturing and service sectors. The margin money (subsidy) is provided upfront and does not need to be repaid. It's implemented through KVIC, KVIBs, and District Industries Centres.

Project & Subsidy Limits

SectorMax Project CostGeneral (Urban)General (Rural)Special Category
Manufacturing₹50 Lakh15%25%35%
Services / Trading₹20 Lakh15%25%35%

Special Category: SC/ST, Women, Ex-servicemen, PWD, NE Region, Hill/Border areas, Minorities

Eligibility

  • Any individual above 18 years of age
  • Minimum 8th pass for projects above ₹10 lakh (mfg) / ₹5 lakh (services)
  • Only new projects — existing units not eligible
  • Self Help Groups (including BPL), charitable trusts, co-operative societies also eligible
  • Applicant must contribute 5–10% as own contribution

How to Apply

  • Visit kviconline.gov.in/pmegpeportal
  • Fill online application with business plan
  • Submit to nearby KVIB/KVIC/DIC office
  • Attend EDP (Entrepreneurship Development Programme) training
  • Bank sanctions loan; subsidy transferred to loan account

Export Finance • Ministry of Commerce

Export Finance Interest Subvention Scheme

3% Interest Subvention on Pre & Post Shipment Credit

About the Scheme

MSMEs engaged in exports receive 3% subvention (reduction) in their interest rate on export credit in Indian Rupees. This is provided on both pre-shipment credit (Packing Credit) and post-shipment credit. Banks claim reimbursement from ECGC/RBI on quarterly basis.

What is Pre & Post Shipment Credit?

  • Pre-shipment (Packing Credit): Short-term credit given to exporters before goods are shipped, to buy raw materials, manufacture, and pack goods
  • Post-shipment: Credit against documents sent for collection after shipment; funds given against shipping bill and BoL

Eligibility

  • Valid Udyam Registration as MSME
  • Active IEC from DGFT
  • Export credit availed from a scheduled commercial bank
  • Account must be regular/Standard
  • Export must be of physical goods (not all service exports covered)

Special Zone • IFSCA

GIFT City — International Financial Services Centre

Tax Incentives for International Financial Business

Key Tax Benefits

  • 100% income tax exemption for any 10 years out of 15 consecutive years
  • No GST on transactions within IFSC
  • No Securities Transaction Tax (STT) on transactions
  • Relaxed FEMA regulations — foreign currency transactions freely allowed
  • Low stamp duty
  • MAT / AMT: 9% for IFSC units (vs 15% normal)

Eligible Activities

  • Banking, insurance, capital markets, fund management
  • Global In-house Centres (GIC)
  • Ship leasing
  • Aircraft leasing
  • Fintech and trading companies

Women Entrepreneur

Women MSME Schemes — Udyogini & Stree Shakti

Special Credit Benefits for Women-Led Businesses

Available Benefits

  • 0.05–0.5% concession in interest rate for women entrepreneurs
  • Enhanced CGTMSE coverage: 80% vs 75% for others
  • SBI Stree Shakti: loans up to ₹50 lakh at 0.50% lower rate if women hold ≥50% stake and attend EDP
  • Udyogini Scheme (State): up to ₹3 lakh collateral-free for specific sectors
  • Priority allotment in government parks and industrial clusters
  • SIDBI Mahila Udyam Nidhi: up to ₹10 lakh for new enterprises

Eligibility

  • Women owning minimum 51% stake in the enterprise
  • Udyam Registration in the woman's name
  • EDP (Entrepreneurship Development Programme) completion may be required for some banks

Technology Upgrade • MSME Ministry

Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme (CLCSS)

15% Capital Subsidy on Technology Upgradation

About CLCSS

CLCSS facilitates technology upgrade in small enterprises by providing 15% upfront capital subsidy on institutional credit up to ₹1 crore for purchase of new plant and machinery. Covers 51 specified sub-sectors ranging from food processing to auto components.

Key Features

  • Subsidy: 15% of eligible loan (max ₹15 lakh)
  • Must purchase well-established and improved technology
  • Only new machinery — second-hand not eligible
  • Implemented through SIDBI, NABARD, and commercial banks
  • 51 specified sub-sectors — check your sector on CLCSS portal
